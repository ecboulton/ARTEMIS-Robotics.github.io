About Us

ARTEMIS is an Australian all-girls team from Abbotsleigh Senior School in Sydney which sees the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) as the kiln that forges our future in areas far greater than just robotics. As a team, ARTEMIS sees FRC through the life changing opportunities and memories we share; our experiences shaping us like clay on a potter’s wheel, preparing us as vessels to carry the legacy of FIRST. In this way we create a fairer world, becoming carriers of STEM in our community.

Our mission statement is “STEM for a fairer world” and we try to embody this in everything we set out to do.

ARTEMIS is an acronym for ‘Abbotsleigh Robotics Team: Engineering, Mathematics, Inspiration and Science’. Our name was inspired by Artemis, the Greek goddess of hunting and wildlife, who is reflected in the arrows in our team logo.

Within ARTEMIS, there is both active FLL and FRC teams. As the FRC team, we help the FLL teams by mentoring them. LEGO® provides the optimum building blocks for FRC as it is easily accessible and has helped us develop a deep interest in robotics and STEM. FLL initialises a bond between robotics and life skills like problem solving and creative thinking.

Through FRC, we can continue to develop these skills in a comfortable and friendly environment. We currently have 4 FLL teams running in years 7 and 8, the majority of whom will go on to join the FRC team. We promote this progression by running an FTC team in the off-season, allowing potential FRC students in year 8 to get a taste of FRC build practices and team members through competing in the FTC competition.
